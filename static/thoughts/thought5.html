STATISCTICAL TESTS FOR PRODUCT MANAGEMENT AND MARKETING

Why use statistical tests?

When growing any conclusions from your data, you want to be sure that

Chi Square test

This test will tell us what are the chances that the difference between Sample A and Sample B is due to chance. Chi square test works for categorical data.
Use-cases:

  • Advertising, testing which ad performs better

  • A/B testing

T test

T test will compare means of 2 samples and tell us if difference between them is significant, is it due to our actions or simply due to chance. T test works for continuous variables like ( page per session, time to action etc. ).
Use cases:

  • Advertising, to compare page views/ order volume / time on site produced by leads that came from Ad 1 vs Ad 2.

  • Product Management, evaluating change in metrics, time to action before and after a design change, retention before and after new push notifications were introduced.

ANOVA (Analysis of Variance)

ANOVA will help us to see if difference in mean values between more more than two samples is significant.
Use cases:

  • Product Management, when testing user groupâ€™s effect on something, does average time in app is different for users that belong to user group A, B, C, D.

  • Advertising, when testing performance of 3 or more ads at the same time.

Turkey - Cramer Procedure

Turkey - Cramer Procedure will tell us which par of samples has a significant difference between their means. Use cases for this one are pretty much the same as for ANOVA.
